Removing white space from the ends of a string

Problem

Remove possible white spaces from the beginning and end of a string.

Solution 

; strip : string -> string
;  remove white space from the beginning and end of a string
(define strip
  (let ([r-strip (regexp "^[ \t\r]*(.*?)[ \t\r]*$")])
    (lambda (s)
      (cadr (regexp-match r-strip s)))))


> (strip " foo ")
"foo"
> (strip " foo bar ")
"foo bar"
> (strip " foo bar")
"foo bar"
> (strip "foo bar")
"foo bar"
> (strip "")
""
> (strip "   ")
""

Discussion

The solution uses a regular expression to pick out the middle of the string. The regular expression is compiled only once, namely when strip is defined.
