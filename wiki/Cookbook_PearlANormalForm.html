Transforming CoreScheme ? into A Normal Form

Compilers for higher order langauges often use different forms of temporary representations of the program.

In A normal form the program source is linearized and all temporary results are named. It is therefore easy to produce assembler from normalized programs.

The linear time algorithm below transforms expressions in CoreScheme ? into A Normal Form. The algorithm is from "The Essence of Compiling with Continuations" by Cormac Flanagan, Amr Sabry, Bruce Duba and Matthias Felleisen. The programming technique used was pionered by Danvy and Filinski.

The abstract syntax for CoreScheme ? and the corresponding A normal form is: 

;; Abstract syntaks for Core Scheme (CS)
     M  ::=   V                      Values
         |    (let (x M) M)
         |    (if0 M M M)
         |    (M M ... M)
         |    (O M ... M)            Primitive Operations
     V  ::=  c                       Constants
         |   x                       Variables
         | (lambda x1 ... xn . M)
;; A-normalized CS
     M  ::=   V                             (return)
         |    (let (x V) M)                 (bind)
         |    (if0 V M M)                   (branch)
         |    (V V1 ... Vn)                 (tail call)
         |    (let ((x (V V1 ... Vn))) M)   (call)
         |    (O V1 ... Vn)                 (prim-op)
         |    (let ((x (O V1 ... Vn))) M)   (prim-op)
     V  ::=  c | x | (lambda x ... x . M)   Values
