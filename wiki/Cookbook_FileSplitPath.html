Splitting a Path into All of Its Parts

Problem

You want to split a path into all of its components.

Solution

explode-path in mzlib's file.ss will do this. 
> (explode-path "C:\\winnt\\system32\\drivers\\etc")
("C:\\" "winnt" "system32" "drivers" "etc")

Discussion

explode-path requires that a path be normalized, i.e. symbolic links resolved and not a relative path. You can use normalize-path to get a normalized path: 

> (current-directory)
"C:\\Program Files\\PLT"
> (explode-path "..\\..\\winnt\\system32\\drivers\\etc")
explode-path: expects argument of type <path in normal form>;
 given "..\\..\\winnt\\system32\\drivers\\etc"
> (explode-path
  (normalize-path "..\\..\\winnt\\system32\\drivers\\etc"))
("C:\\" "winnt" "system32" "drivers" "etc")
